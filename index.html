Music

High school was a time of self-discovery for me. I learned a lot about myself, my likes, my dislikes, and really what makes me who I am in general. It was during this time that I discovered the band The Minibosses. They performed rock and roll covers of music from old video games that I played when I was a kid. I remember thinking to myself, “If they can do stuff like that, then why can’t I?” I taught myself how to transcribe music in order to arrange music from NES games for the piano, and then I taught myself how to play the piano in order to play the music I arranged.

It was during this time that I decided to enroll in AP music theory at my high school (that itself is a whole other story for another day). I quickly found that I was at a major disadvantage compared to the other students: each one of them had been in band for many years, and had a lot more experience with music than I did. I was blessed to have some very helpful classmates who me get down the basics that I needed to keep going in the class. I eventually began singing and writing music of my own. Music became the outlet that I was looking for during my high school years. Here are a few samples of some my compositions/recordings:

  • Agony – In college, I auditioned for my university’s opera program as a joke, and (much to my surprise) I was cast as a lead. I decided to keep up with opera, and have performed in various opera scene programs at my university. This is a recording of "Agony" from Sondheim's Into the Woods. I performed with my friend Josh at one of those opera programs (mine is the first of the two voices you’ll hear).
  • Sky Song – A few years back I decided to start experimenting chip tune sounds. There's something about those 8-bit beats that just gets the blood flowing. (Note: this song is kind of loud, so you may need to turn down your speakers before starting it.)
  • Instruments: Piano, Voice
  • Influences: Nobuo Uematsu, Frédéric Chopin, Styx

Food Science

As a high school student, I dreamed of becoming a chef. I fell in love with food science, and, often to my mom's dismay, often took to experimenting in the kitchen. Years later while I was engaged to my sweet wife, both she and her mother were diagnosed with food allergies (specifically to dairy, eggs, wheat, soy, and peanuts). Through hours of slaving over a hot stove (and even more hours researching various topics in food science), I've created a collection of allergen-friendly desserts for everything from chocolate mousse to orange sherbet.

I love to talk food (especially food science) and share my findings. My food idol is Alton Brown and I (proudly) admit that I've seen every episode of Good Eats. Feel free to contact me if you're interested in some alternative recipes.

  • Specialty: Allergen-free desserts
  • Latest recipe: Vegan peppermint chip ice cream

Software Development

Growing up, I always had a knack for math. I was the kid in elementary school who did logic puzzles for fun (only when dodgeball wasn't an option, naturally). During my first few years of college, I changed majors several times. Eventually, I took an introductory computer science course. A few weeks into the class (after the typical "what am I getting myself into" panic), I found myself daydreaming about all the cool things I wanted to code up.

That's when I knew I had found my new home.

Ever since then, I've been hooked. Whether it be designing an algorithm or gathering requirements for a new project, I love all aspects of software engineering.


Projects
