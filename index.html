Food Science

When I was in high school, I dreamed of being a chef. I fell in love with food science and, often to my mom's dismay, often took to experimenting in the kitchen. My food idol is Alton Brown and I (proudly) admit that I've seen every episode of Good Eats. Now, several years later, several of my family members have been diagnosed with food allergies (specifically to dairy, eggs, wheat, soy, and peanuts). Through hours of slaving over a hot stove, I've created a collection of allergen-friendly desserts for everything from chocolate mousse pie to orange sherbet. I love to talk food (especially food science) and share my findings. Feel free to contact me if you're interested in some alternative recipes.

Software Development

Growing up, I always had a knack for math. I was the kid in elementary school who did logic puzzles for fun (only when dodgeball wasn't an option, naturally). During my first few years of college, I changed majors several times. Eventually, I took an introductory computer science course. A few weeks into the class (after the typical "what am I getting myself into" panic), I found myself daydreaming about all the cool things I wanted to code up.

That's when I knew I had found my new home.

Ever since then, I've been hooked. Whether it be designing an algorithm or gathering requirements for a new project, I love all aspects of software engineering. I'm currently working on a 3D game using Three.js. You can check out my progress here!

  • Github: ajrind
  • Languages: Java, C++, JavaScript, Python, HTML, CSS, AngularJS, SQL, and R
